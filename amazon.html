Amazon FinTech

During my summer internship at Amazon, I worked on two projects as part of the FinTech team. The first project was to create a centralized repository that makes it easier for customers to learn about payment related products and services. The second project was to create a new product that helps teams automate the process for addressing tickets and issues that arise during the payments process.

Timeline: May 2022 - Aug 2022

Tools: Figma

Project Type: Internship

Team: 1 PM, 1 Designer, 2 Engineers, 12 Stakeholders

Process

  • User research interviews
  • Propose user flow and information architecture
  • Low fidelity wireframes
  • Design iterations based on stakeholder feedback
  • Mid fidelity wireframes & prototypes
  • Design iterations based on stakeholder & user feedback
  • Final high fidelity wireframes & prototypes
  • Documentation & handoff to engineers
  • Final presentation

Key Takeaways

Advocating for the User

As I balanced the needs and expectations of the product manager and engineers, I learned how to advocate for the user. I improved at explaining the reasoning behind design decisions and supporting my decisions with evidence from user testing and/or user research.

Design for Technical Feasability

For my first project when I was improving an existing product, I learned that my design while in theory, worked well for the user, had potential to worsen the user experience as it would significantly slow down run time. I learned to expand my technical considerations and think about things such as number of queries to the database.

Thinking Long-Term

As I worked on my second project which involved designing a product from scratch, I learned how important it is to plan long-term and envision what the product could be six months to two years from now.
