Hello!

I'm Ishika, a designer who codes. I'm a self-taught product designer, who loves to solve problems and design experiences that are intuitive and accessible. Currently, I'm a junior at Cornell, studying Information Science with a concentration in UX, and a minor in Business. I am a Milstein Scholar and Vice President of Academic for Women in Computing at Cornell.

Currently, I'm a Technical Advisor Intern at IBM where I'm designing and building a data visualization dashboard, Product Designer at Pavvilion - a startup I co-founded, and Product Research Lead at Hack4Impact where I focus on building tech solutions for non-profits.

Since high school, I've been passionate about social change and have tried to make a difference in the community, whether it be by performing and organizing concerts for senior citizens or writing about social issues. I recently discovered the intersection of technology and social impact through my Google Classroom Case Study - design. And I fell in love with it. Since then, I've been challenging myself to learn more and more by designing across platforms including the web, mobile devices, and virtual reality.

I'm always excited for new opportunities to grow as a designer and collaborate with others to create beautiful digital experiences. Let's connect!
