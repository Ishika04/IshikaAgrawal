Pavvilion (MVP)

In February of my internship at the Cornell Tech Policy Lab, I was tasked to create a new social media app geared towards college students. Working closely with a team of designers and engineers, I helped design Pavvilion which provides a centralized location for students to find opportunities, meet people with similar interests, and find clubs and organizations that fit their preferences.

Timeline: Feb. 2021 - Present

Tools: Figma, Whimsical

Project Type: Startup

Team: 2 PM, 2 Designers, 1 Engineer

Key Takeaways

Iterate Quickly and Often

At first, my co-designer and I worked on trying to create the "perfect" design, which led us to getting stuck such that our designs weren't evolving fast enough. Once we started focusing on quantity rather than quality, I found that the quality also improved as we came up with many new ideas for the designs that we hadn't thought of before.

Design for Engineering Feasability

Oftentimes, I had to go back to simplify the UI to ensure that the engineers would be able to keep up with the timeline when building the MVP. I learned that in the first iterations especially, feasability should be emphasized in the designs.

Consistency is Key

This was the first time that I'd worked extensively with another designer to create a product. Because my partner and I had such different styles, it was important to make sure that our final designs were consistent. It was helpful to establish a design system and guidelines early on.

Understanding the User

Since the task was very broad, it was important to gain a better understanding of the social scene on campus and the challenges that students face in trying to navigate it. We conducted several interviews with individuals at Cornell and other colleges. Our goals were to:

  • Understand how users make friends and find people who share the same interests
  • Discover how students find information on campus as underclassmen vs. upperclassmen
  • Uncover pain points and product opportunities

Mapping the User Journey

After conducting user research interviews, I considered how the user's experience evolves as they go through the entire process of searching for clubs and events, recruitment, keeping up with club events, and advertising events. I identified the user's thoughts, emotions, key pain points, and opportunity areas for Pavvilion to address.





Key Findings

Empathizing with the Target Users

To really understand the target users better, I created a couple of personas so that I could relate more to the user’s needs, wants, thoughts, and priorities.



Key Takeaways:

Upperclassmen Student (Clarrissa)

  • Have an established friend group on campus and are part of clubs that relate to their interests
  • Want to get to know more people but are less likely to take the initiative to reach out
  • Find out about opportunities on campus by word of mouth or based on who they follow on social media

Underclassmen Student (Chris)

  • Are actively looking to meet new people and are willing to go through competitive club recruitment processes to find interest groups
  • Will go out of their way to reach out to people in class or from clubs to meet up with them
  • Find out about opportunities on campus via campus events (like Club Fair) or based on posters/signs

Pinpointing the Problem

After learning how students currently interact and learn about on campus clubs and events through user research interviews, I learned that there are several pain points in this process. Here are some of the key issues:

Club and Event Information is Scattered

There is no centralized location on campus to access all of the information you need. Students learn about clubs and events via social media (Facebook, Reddit, Instagram), campus events (Club Fair, tabling events), word of mouth, and physical advertising (flyers, posters, chalk on sidewalks). Because the process of finding these clubs is spontaneous and not systematic, students have a hard time finding groups and events that fit their interests.

Hard to Gage Club Culture

Before students decide to go to an event or join a club, there's a lot of information that they consider than can be difficult to gage without interacting with members themselves. Some of these items include commitment level, competitiveness, extent of collaboration, how close-knit members are, mentorship opportunities, and other intangible qualities that are difficult to measure.

Campus Involvement Requires Extensive Planning

Attending club events or hosting events requires a lot of planning to advertise. To advertise, students have to make designs, contact other organizations, and consider how they'll advertise across platforms. For attendees, they have to keep track of event dates and find people to go with. And during recruitment especially, they keep track of application deadlines, interview dates, and events.

Exploring Possible Solutions

Before brainstorming with the team, I first looked into existing solutions within the market to understand what other solutions do well and where they fall short.

Considering Existing Market Solutions

I looked at both companies that directly address the main problem such as CampusGroups and ClubView as well as those that indirectly solve this such as Reddit and Facebook.


Through this analysis, I learned that there's no product in the market that centrally serves all of the needs that student leaders who are recruiting and students who are searching for clubs/events.

Narrowing Down to 3 Solutions

After brainstorming, I decided to narrow down on 3 potential solutions:


1) Coffee Chats

Students who share similar interests are paired together for a weekly coffee chat



2) Interest Groups

Students can join group chats based on the topics that they are most interested in (e.g. a group chat for country singers)



3) Information Board

Students and clubs can post any information that can be seen by the entire college community

Pursuing a Solution

I initially loved the coffee chats feature, as it makes learning about a new club or making new friends a more personal experience where you are guaranteed to have interests in common with whom you are matched with. After I sketched out more ideas though, I felt that the information board best solves the challenge that students are facing on campus.

Thus, I decided to move forward with the INFORMATION BOARD because:

  1. It gives both upperclassmen a platform to spread the word about information more quickly and underclassmen a way to quickly learn about information.
  2. It can minimize the amount of apps users open use as they research clubs or look for other opportunities or information.
  3. It caters to students with content that is student-driven.
  4. Provides insight into non-tangible aspects that students look for like club culture and commitment level.

Pavvilion's Key Features

After deciding to move forward with the Information Board as our key feature, we outlined some of the other features that we wanted going forward within the app. Here is a quick overview:

Information Board: Tune Into What Your Peers Are Discussing

A method for students to explore new items and send out messages to the entire campus community. Advantages:

  • Learn about all opportunities from one place
  • Send events out to a broader audience to increase attendance
  • Sort by interests to find relevant topics while learning what's trending on your campus

Profiles: Learn About a Club from 1 Place

A simple way of consolidating information that was scattered across multiple platforms. The profile page makes it easy to find recruitment information, upcoming events, previous posts, a description of the club, and a list of the members.

  • Helps students manage recruitment applications & deadlines
  • Consolidates all information in one place

My Page: Personalized Summary Page of Posts & Events (Coming Soon)

A way for students to quickly figure out what content the clubs they're in are posting so that they can stay up-to-date on what's going on, while also being able to see their upcoming events.

  • Eliminates the need to be on multiple listervs
  • Gives the user more control on what clubs are contacting and sending information

Events: Get Involved on Campus (Coming Soon)

A more approachable way for students to find events that they might be interested in, both within the clubs they're in and public events.

  • Provides opportunities for spontaneous interactions
  • Sorts content by interest to recommend relevant events
  • More approachable and less overwhelming than competitors

Next Steps

This was my first time building an app from scratch — starting with user research all the way to determining branding and launching. Through this process, I learned how important it is to be able to justify every design decision and that oftentimes the first solution you come up with is going to be very different from the one you end up with as you continue to iterate.

User Testing & Iterating Based on Feedback

Over the last few weeks, we have have started conducted user testing. Based on the feedback that we have been receiving, we will continue to iterate on the app and address any of the issues that come up.

Flesh Out Features for Iteration 2

For each iteration, we came up with a least of new features that we were going to implement. The next steps are to expand on the MVP and build in the new features that were originally planned.
